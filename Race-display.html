    • - Valar -

    The Valar are the powerful Ainur who shaped the world of Arda, each overseeing aspects of its creation and governance, embodying immense might and wisdom in J.R.R. Tolkien's legendarium.

    - Maiar -

    In Tolkien's world, the Maiar are lesser Ainur, akin to angels, who served the Valar and could take physical form in Middle-Earth, often assisting and guiding its inhabitants, sometimes even influencing events in subtle or significant ways.

    - Elf -

    Elves are an ancient, wise, and beautiful race with immortality, renowned for their connection to nature, skill in crafts, and residence in places like Rivendell or Lothlórien.

    - Dwarf -

    Dwarves are stout, hardy beings in Tolkien's world, known for their craftsmanship, mining skills, and love for riches and treasure.

    - Men -

    Humans in Tolkien's world, often referred to as Men, are mortal beings with great diversity in culture, ambition, and capacity for both good and evil.

    - Hobbit -

    Hobbits are a diminutive, peace-loving folk known for their love of simple pleasures, good food, and comfortable living. They value friendship, loyalty, and a quiet life, often found in the pastoral settings of the Shire.

    - Orc -

    The noble and immortal race of Elves in Tolkien's world was tragically twisted and corrupted by the dark powers of Morgoth into the malevolent Orcs, a brutal and cruel race known for their ferocity and lack of compassion in Middle-earth's conflicts.
